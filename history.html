A little bit of the monkees' History

The members of the band were selected among more than 500 young people (among the rejected were musicians who were later famous
as Stephen Stills and Danny Hutton, since the producers were more interested in creating a commercial concept than having talented musicians
to play or compose music, despite this Michael Nesmith managed from the first album to integrate his compositions) that went to the auditions
for a television show and records produced by Don Kirshner. The show had an irreverent sense of humor, very similar or rather copied to
"A Hard Day's Night", the first movie starring The Beatles; besides this only two of them were musicians (Michael Nesmith and Peter Tork)
and the rest learned to play on the fly, while in their first albums the track was played and composed by the best musicians and composers
of the United States like Carole King, Neil Sedaka, Neil Diamond, Tommy Boice and Bobby Hart, so there could be no failure in the aspect
of success and between 1966 and 1968 there was not.

From the first moment the talent of Nesmith was coming to the light and in each disc of the band his compositions were gaining weight,
getting to have five songs of his in the disc Headquarters. The rest of the group was also collaborating little by little with their own subjects.
From the Headquarters album they already had the ability to play the instruments on the disc and they did so. Unfortunately for them
The Beatles album Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band went on sale and they were displaced from number 1 after just one week.
In addition they undertook next to Jack Nicholson the project of the film "Head", that was a disaster; his program was canceled and soon
Michael Nesmith left the band realizing that for its creators the Monkees were only a product and nothing more. Later Nesmith would have
an interesting solo career and with the First National Band as a precursor of the Country Rock style.

Interestingly in this era The Monkees refused to record "Sugar Sugar" and Kirshner to see the possibility of saving the selection and conflicts
that led to choose the members, created a cartoon group called The Archies, based on the comic strip Archie whose name the song was published.
"Sugar Sugar" was the biggest success of 1969.

The Monkees had blockbusters like "Last train to Clarkville", "I'm a Believer", "She", "I'm Not Your Steepin 'Stone", "A Little Bit Me A Little Bit You",
"Sweet Young Thing","Look Out Here Comes Tomorrow","Pleasent Valley Sunday","When Loves Come Knockin 'At Your Door","I Wanna Be Free",
"Theme Of The Monkees", "Daydream Believer" and in 1986 they met and had another important success "That Was Then, This Is Now.
